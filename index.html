Problem #1

Inventory Is Managed From Memory

Most restaurant owners track stock in their head or in a basic spreadsheet they barely update. When things run out mid-service, it is already too late.

There is no alert. No system. No visibility. Just the moment you realise the kitchen is out of something critical.

💸

Problem #2

Money Is Lost to Waste and Over-Ordering

Without tracking, the same item gets bought twice. Fresh produce expires unnoticed. Stock builds up in one corner while the kitchen runs short on something else.

These are not big one-time losses — they are small daily leaks that add up to a significant amount every month.

How It Works

Just Send a Message. StockBot Does the Rest.

No training. No new app. No spreadsheet to maintain. The owner chats with StockBot the same way they message anyone on Telegram.

1

Check Stock in One Message

The owner types a single word — "restock list" or "stock status" — and StockBot reads the live inventory and replies instantly. No opening a spreadsheet, no scrolling through rows.

  • Restock list — items below minimum, sorted by urgency
  • Stock status — full category-wise summary
  • Low stock — what is running out right now
  • Any specific item — "how much rice do we have?"
The bot reads your live Google Sheet. Every answer reflects the actual current stock — not a guess.
2

Update Stock Through Conversation

When stock arrives or gets used, the owner just says so. StockBot understands natural language — in English or Hindi — and updates the inventory automatically after confirming the change.

  • Add new stock — "I bought 10kg rice today"
  • Log usage — "we used 2kg sambar powder"
  • Add new items — "add Diet Coke, 24 bottles, minimum 12"
  • Update minimum thresholds — "set minimum for oil to 5L"
  • Understands local names — haldi, jeera, rai, hing
The bot always confirms before writing anything. Nothing changes without the owner's approval.
3

Get Alerts Without Asking

Every evening at 9pm, StockBot automatically checks the inventory and sends a daily report. The owner does not need to ask — it just arrives.

  • Daily 9pm alert — low stock and zero stock items
  • Triggered instantly when any item drops below minimum
  • Clean, readable summary — no noise, just what matters
The owner sees the problem before service starts — not during it.
4

The Spreadsheet Stays Clean Automatically

Every update the bot makes goes straight into a Google Sheet the owner can view at any time. The Dashboard sheet gives a live overview — total items, what needs restocking, and health by category.
